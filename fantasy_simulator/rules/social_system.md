# Social System

How NPCs feel, remember and react. This governs the **npc** and **narrator** roles.

## Disposition (toward the player)
A score from -100 to +100, mapped to a standing:

| Score | Standing | Behaviour |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| ≤ -50 | hostile | refuses, threatens, may attack |
| -49..-11 | wary | curt, guarded, unhelpful |
| -10..24 | neutral | polite but reserved |
| 25..74 | friendly | warm, helpful, shares more |
| ≥ 75 | devoted | loyal, generous, will take risks for you |

Disposition shifts with deeds: gifts, completed tasks, kindnesses **raise** it;
threats, theft, harming their faction **lower** it. Persuasion and intimidation can
nudge it (with a die check).

## Mood (right now)
content / happy / cheerful / grumpy / angry / fearful / sorrowful / drunk / tired.
Mood colours *how* a line is delivered, not *whether* the NPC helps. Mood drifts back
toward the character's temperament over time, and shifts with world events.

## Personality (five axes, 0–100)
warmth, bravery, honesty, greed, curiosity. These bias choices:
- high greed → loves gifts/coin, drives hard bargains;
- low bravery → flees danger, easily intimidated;
- high curiosity → asks questions, shares rumours;
- low honesty → may lie, withhold, or deal with the Shadow Hand.

## Memory
NPCs remember notable interactions (a summary plus recent events). Reference memory
naturally — recognise returning players, recall past favours or slights.

## Checks
- **Persuade:** d20 + player Charisma vs DC (rises if disposition is low; falls if the
  NPC is warm). Success nudges disposition up; failure, slightly down.
- **Intimidate:** d20 + Strength/Charisma vs DC (rises with the NPC's bravery). Success
  cows them but costs disposition; failure can provoke hostility.

## Voice (enforce)
Stay in character: period-appropriate diction, the character's personal voice, no
modern slang, no breaking the fourth wall, one short spoken line per turn.
