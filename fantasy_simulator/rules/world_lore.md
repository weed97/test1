# World Lore — The Realm of Aldermere

## Setting
A late-medieval fantasy kingdom at uneasy peace. Steel, candlelight, faith and a
guarded, dangerous magic. No gunpowder, no modern technology.

## The realm at a glance
- **The Verdant Vale** — heartland farmland and the town of **Brackenford** (tavern,
  smithy, market, temple, inn). Safe, ruled by the Crown.
- **Thornwood** — a dark forest of wolves, giant spiders and the **Redhand** bandits;
  a forgotten shrine festers within.
- **The Frostpeak Range** — mines, ice passes, and the roost of **Skorvaxis, the
  Frostpeak Wyrm**, a dragon asleep for three hundred years.
- **Mirewater Fen** — a brooding marsh, home to the hedge-witch **Morwenna**, drowned
  ruins and restless dead.
- **Highcrown** — the white-walled capital: the grand bazaar, the Cathedral of the
  Dawn, the Arcane Conclave, and the Keep of the Crown.

## The great powers (factions)
- **The Crown of Aldermere** — law and knights. Rivals: bandits, thieves.
- **The Merchant Coalition** — wealthy trade guild.
- **The Arcane Conclave** — keepers of magical lore.
- **The Order of the Dawn** — faith of light and healing.
- **The Shadow Hand** — a thieves' guild in the cracks of society.
- **The Redhand Brigands** — raiders of the roads (hostile).
- **The Fenfolk** — reclusive marsh-dwellers.

## The central thread
Omens gather: an eclipse, ill dreams, the Conclave's warnings. **The Wyrm is waking.**
The Crown will ennoble whoever can end the threat. Lesser troubles — bandits on the
roads, sickness, the restless dead of the Fen — ripple outward from this rising dread.

## Tone
Grounded and human. Ordinary folk with ordinary worries living beside extraordinary
danger. Hope, grit, and dark humour. Avoid anachronism and modern slang.
